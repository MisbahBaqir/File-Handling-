  The Seerat of Prophet Muhammad (s.a.w) is extensive

  1. Birth and Early Life: Prophet Muhammad was born in Mecca in the year 570 CE. Orphaned at an early age, he was raised by his grandfather and later by his uncle.

  2. Prophethood: At the age of 40, Muhammad received his first revelation from Allah through the Angel Gabriel. Over the next 23 years, he continued to receive revelations, which were later compiled into the Quran.

  3. Opposition in Mecca: The early years of Prophethood were marked by opposition and persecution from the Quraysh tribe in Mecca, as the message of monotheism challenged their existing beliefs.

  4. Migration to Medina (Hijra): In 622 CE, facing increasing hostility, Prophet Muhammad and his followers migrated to Medina. This event marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

  5. Establishment of the Islamic State: In Medina, Prophet Muhammad established a cohesive Muslim community and played a role in resolving conflicts among various tribes.

  6. Conquests and Challenges: Over the next decade, the Muslims faced challenges and engaged in battles with the Quraysh and other opposing forces. Notable events include the Battle of Badr, Battle of Uhud, and the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah.

  7. Conquest of Mecca: In 630 CE, Prophet Muhammad peacefully entered Mecca, cleansing the Kaaba of idols and establishing Islam in the city.

  8. Farewell Pilgrimage: In 632 CE, Prophet Muhammad performed his final pilgrimage, where he delivered his famous Farewell Sermon, emphasizing equality and unity among Muslims.

  9. Death: Prophet Muhammad passed away in 632 CE in Medina. His death marked the end of the era of revelation, but his legacy continued through the Quran and the Sunnah (his teachings and actions).
